Abstract de Honduras

Honduras became independent from Spain in 1821, but in 1822 it was united with Mexico and four other nations, Costa Rica, El Salvador, Guatemala and Nicaragua. However, the five Central American nations seceded from Mexico in 1823. Honduras finally became fully independent in 1839. At the beginning of the 20th century, banana exports came to dominate the Honduran economy. In 1932 General Tiburcio Carias Andino was elected president of Honduras. He became a virtual dictator and remained in power until 1949. General Oswaldo López took power in Honduras in 1963. Then, in 1969, Honduras fought a war with El Salvador. Oswaldo López resigned in 1974, but civilian government was not restored until 1981. Unfortunately, Honduras suffered greatly when Hurricane Mitch hit the country in 1998. However, the country slowly recovered. However, Honduras remains a very poor country. Today the population of Honduras is 9.1 million inhabitants.
