ESTADOS UNIDOS PAÍS DEL BALONCESTO

Estados Unidos es un país de 50 estados que ocupa una extensa franja de América del Norte, con Alaska en el noroeste y Hawái que extiende la presencia del país en el océano Pacífico. Entre las principales ciudades de la costa del Atlántico, se encuentran Nueva York, un centro global financiero y cultural, y la capital Washington D.C. Chicago, metrópolis del medio oeste, es famosa por su influencia arquitectónica y, en la costa oeste, Hollywood, Los Ángeles, es famosa por la industria cinematográfica.

POBLACIÓN DE ESTADOS UNIDOS

Estados Unidos entrará al año 2024 con 335.893.238 habitantes, un incremento del 0,53 % sobre la población un año antes, según el cálculo divulgado este jueves por la Oficina del Censo.

ACTIVIDAD ECONÓMICA

El sector de servicios es, con gran diferencia, el que cuenta con mayor peso dentro de la economía estadounidense. En 2021, este sector representó el 77,6% del PIB del país. En contraste, el sector agrícola contribuyó con menos del 1% al PIB estadounidense. Estados Unidos es la mayor economía del mundo por delante de China. Tras una década de crecimiento, la tasa de crecimiento del PIB del país pasó a ser negativa a raíz de la pandemia de COVID-19, agravada por el aumento de las desigualdades y la obsolescencia de las infraestructuras.

The United States, a nation located in North America, is recognized for its cultural diversity, vibrant economy, and global influence on politics, technology, culture, and the economy. With a diverse population of more than 300 million, the country is made up of 50 states and one federal district, Washington D.C. The United States economy is one of the largest in the world, based primarily on the free market and innovation. It is a leader in industries such as technology, medicine, entertainment and finance. However, wealth distribution and socioeconomic disparities are important issues in the country. In terms of politics, the United States is a constitutional republic with a federal government system made up of three branches: legislative, executive, and judicial. The president, elected every four years, is the head of state and government. Congress, made up of the Senate and the House of Representatives, is responsible for creating laws and approving the federal budget. American culture is diverse and dynamic, influenced by a mix of indigenous, European, African, and Asian traditions. Free speech and religious freedom are fundamental values, and American popular culture is exported around the world through movies, music, television, and technology. Despite its global prominence, the United States faces significant challenges, including political polarization, economic inequality, climate change, and racial and social tensions. However, the country remains a world power with a central role on the international stage. This abstract provides an overview of the United States, addressing key aspects such as its economy, politics, culture, and current challenges.
